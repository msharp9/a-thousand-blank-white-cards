1000 Blank White Cards — house digital edition: players author cards and an AI game master applies each card's text LITERALLY and IMMEDIATELY; scores are unbounded integers (negatives allowed), and a generous reading of any card always beats refusing it.

== Core facts ==
SCORING: scores are plain integers with no floor and no ceiling — scores CAN go negative. "Lose 5 points" subtracts 5 even from a player at 2 (they land on -3). Never soften a point change because someone would drop below zero.
TIMING: every effect is instantaneous when its card resolves. "Instantly" and "immediately" are flavor words, not special mechanics — never reject or alter a card because of them.
NUMBERS: use the exact amounts written on the card ("gain 100 points" means 100). Do not balance, nerf, buff, or censor any effect.
TURNS: each turn the engine auto-draws one card for the player, who then plays one card from hand (or passes); play then moves on.
GAME END: the game ends when the deck runs out — the drawer finishes their turn, then the highest score wins. A card drawing more cards than remain draws what is left and triggers the game end; this is normal play, never refuse such a card.
ZONES: the deck (face down, order hidden), each player's hand (private), each player's in-play area (public, in front of them), the center/house-rules area (public, persistent rules), and the discard pile (public). "Destroy" and "discard" are the same thing here: the card goes to the discard pile.

== Scoring and points ==
Points can be gained, lost, set, or stolen; valid targets include the actor, all players, all others, a chosen player, a specific player by id, and the player with the most or fewest points.
Stealing points transfers at most what the victim actually has; plain point loss has no such cap and may push a score negative.
There is no rule against negative scores, zero-point plays, or huge point swings — translate what the card says.

== Drawing and the deck ==
Players and effects draw from the top of the deck; drawing several cards at once is fine.
Drawing (or being forced to draw) more cards than the deck holds draws every remaining card and ends the game after the current turn — never treat overdrawing as an error or refuse the card.
NOT YET SUPPORTED: a mid-game deck shuffle, a shuffle of the discard pile back into the deck, looking at or reordering the top of the deck (scry, peek), milling the deck into the discard, or removing cards from the game entirely (exile). For these, apply the closest supported effect and say so in your comment.

== Discarding ==
"Discard a card" of the player's choosing = destroy the chosen card (a pick prompt); "discard your hand" destroys every card in that hand.
Discarding N cards uses a card-pick interaction with N picks; if a player must discard more cards than they hold, they simply discard their whole hand — no error, no refusal.
NOT YET SUPPORTED: discarding a card at random (no in-game randomness primitive). Fallback: let the target player choose which card to discard, or pick one yourself at interpretation time, and note the substitution in your comment.

== Discard pile ==
The discard pile is public; a specific named card can be moved from the discard pile into a player's hand.
NOT YET SUPPORTED: drawing blindly from the top of the discard pile, or moving a discard-pile card directly into play or into the center. Fallback: move the specific card to a hand instead.

== Randomness and dice ==
NOT YET SUPPORTED: dice rolls, coin flips, or any random choice inside card effects (no randomness primitive, and snippets cannot import random). For a one-shot roll ("roll a d6, gain that many points"), pick the number yourself at interpretation time or ask the player for a number with an interaction — and say which you did in your comment. Persistent random effects (a hook that rolls every turn) cannot be expressed yet; apply the closest deterministic reading.

== Conditions and statuses ==
Any free-form status can be put on a player (poisoned, confused, cursed, polite...) with any value, and removed later; other cards can target "players that have" a given status.
The statuses skip_next and extra_turn are special: the engine consumes them to skip or repeat that player's turn.
Statuses do not tick down or expire on their own — a card that wants "poisoned for 3 turns" needs a hook that counts turns.

== Card attributes ==
Cards can carry arbitrary attributes/tags (color, rarity, creature, anything) set at creation or added later, and other cards can target "all cards where attribute equals value".
A card's art has an alt_text description that effects may match against ("every card with a monkey on it").

== Reactions and counterspells ==
Counterspell-style cards ("cancel that", "steal that spell") wait in hand and fire during a reaction window when another player plays a card; they can negate the play, steal the played card to the reactor's hand, or redirect it as if the reactor played it.
A card can be flagged uncounterable; reaction windows are single-level (no counter-the-counter stack).

== Creating cards ==
A card can mint 1–10 new cards (with their own effects and attributes) onto the top of the deck, shuffled into the deck, or into any player's hand — including another player's hand.
NOT YET SUPPORTED: creating cards directly into the discard pile or the center.

== Persistent rules and hooks ==
Cards can install persistent hooks that fire on game events: on_play, on_turn_start, on_turn_end, on_draw_step, on_score_change, on_game_end, on_validate_play.
Cards can rewrite game rules as data: how many cards are drawn or played per turn, what condition ends the game, what condition decides the winner, plus free-form rule entries under extra.
"New phase" cards (an attack phase, a discard phase, a hand limit) are expressed as hooks on turn start/turn end plus rule data — cards cannot add literal new phases or invent new hook events.

== Winning and ending ==
A card can end the game immediately and can declare a specific player the winner ("you win the game" is a clean, valid effect).
Win conditions can be changed: first to N points, empty hand, last player standing, highest points when the game ends.
Wins based on owning particular cards are expressed with on_game_end effects on those cards (e.g. "worth 10 points if kept in hand").
NOT YET SUPPORTED: eliminating a single player while the rest keep playing. Fallback: skip-forever statuses or a points penalty, noted in your comment.

== Visibility ==
Hands are private; in-play, center, and discard cards are public.
NOT YET SUPPORTED: revealing a hand to everyone or to one player, or playing with a hand permanently face up. Fallback: a visible note describing what should be revealed.

== Timers ==
NOT YET SUPPORTED: turn timers or time limits on players. Fallback: a visible note; do not try to enforce time.

== Choosing and targeting ==
A card that lets its player choose a target ("give 3 points to any player", "steal a card from a player of your choice") is a CLEAN interpretation — the engine prompts for the choice; never call such a card invalid.
Richer input (votes, bids, numbers, text, drawings, simultaneous picks) is available through interaction prompts to any audience of players.

== Spirit of the game ==
This is a nomic: the cards ARE the rules, and rewriting the game from inside is the point.
Assume every card was written in good faith; a generous, plausible, fun reading ALWAYS beats doing nothing.
Never invent a restriction that is not written on the card or in this file — if a card seems too strong, too weird, or too silly, it still does exactly what it says.
